
Running: You’ll often hear a lot about it. Stuff like how it benefits the body and brain—how it strengthens the heart, builds muscular endurance, enhances neuroplasticity, and sharpens cognition. All of it is true, but to me, those are just trivial details maybe at best some footnotes. 

In some quiet, inexplicable way, I find the act deeply spiritual. It's a raw connection between me and my physiological machine, unmediated, and quietly sacred. As I stride forward, the world falls away—the noise dims, what's left is just me, the rasp of my breath, the beats of my heart—loud, insistent, alive. What could be more real than this—this stripped-down, blood-rushed certainty of being?

When the trail starts, it is magic and curiosity, in the end it is a miracle of discovery and joy. I am no longer running. I am undoing. I am becoming. I am changing.  Throughout, it brings an understanding of what Blake meant when he said, “Energy is eternal delight"
